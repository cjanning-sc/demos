An organization within ACME Engineering’s Payload and Ground Systems (PGS) division, SYNOPTICS is the world-wide leader in the growth, fabrication, and coating of crystalline materials used in laser systems. These “solid-state” laser crystals are used in products ranging from medical lasers to military laser systems. The Charlotte facility has been operating for over 40 years in the laser industry and has developed the reputation of providing the highest quality products with timely deliveries. Within the facility, solid-state oxide and fluoride crystals are grown from a high-temperature melt. SYNOPTICS has full optical fabrication capabilities for various plano and spherical geometries. SYNOPTICS serves the photonics and optics industry across the globe. 

  

Learn more about SYNOPTICS at our website: https://www.acmeengineering.com/space/synoptics/. 

Position Overview/Job Functions

• Primary responsibility will be for operating multiple CNC mills to core drill crystal rods per specification and production schedule. This includes:
    - Generating basic G-code in CAM software and making minor revisions as needed
    - Selecting and maintaining tooling
    - Performing preventative maintenance for equipment
• Follow technical work instructions, understand engineering blueprints, and verify conformance to specification within given tolerances
• Maintain a safe, clean, and organized work area
• Utilize the electronic Shop Floor Control system for data entry and scanning parts through applicable routing steps
• Perform work in a manner complying with our ISO 9001 quality and EHS management systems
• Over time, there will be opportunities to cross-train in all aspects of crystal fabrication, including the operation of grinding, slicing, and other fabrication related equipment

Basic Qualifications:
    - High school diploma or GED
    - Basic computer skills using Microsoft Excel and Word
    - Effective communication skills with peers, engineering, and management are essential
    - Mechanical aptitude, strong work ethic, ability to multitask, positive attitude, and willingness to learn
    - Manual or CNC Machining experience with milling, turning, surface grinding, or centerless grinding
    - Good visual acuity, strong basic math skills, and attention to detail
    - Ability to use hand tools, micrometers and calipers, work in metric or English units, read detailed instructions, interpret engineering prints, maintain written records, and keep an organized work environment using 5S principles


Preferred Qualifications:
    - An associate degree or two-year technical degree
    - Experience with the following equipment is strongly preferred:  HAAS (mill or lathe), Harig, Chevalier, Okamoto, Glebar, or DCM.
    - Experience with Esprit or other CAM software
