F-35 EODAS Chief Engineer

This position is for the Chief Engineer role of the F-35 EODAS program. This postion can be based in Baltimore (Linthicum), MD or Rolling Meadows, IL with the ability for some remote work.

A chief engineer is the definitive technical authority for all aspects of engineering on all program elements (hardware/software/support/test) and leads a multi disciplined engineering team managing the technical performance of the program meeting cost and schedule requirements including subcontracted elements. Ensures that the program is adhering to sound engineering processes in the development, production, test and sustainment of products for our customers and is compliance with company policies, procedures, and quality standards. Maintains cognizance over technical activities within program IPTs and ensures horizontal integration of the best engineering practices across the company. Continually coordinates with the systems engineering and program integration team (PIT) leader to ensure the execution of program statement of work and baselines and serves as the technical point of contact with the customer. There is one Chief Engineer per program.

F-35 EODAS Program Roles and Responsibilities:

  • CAM Responsibilities for Chief, Systems Engineering, Configuration Management, Lab Support
  • Provide Customer support, and meetings with industry leadership for Return to Green plans.  Currently EODAS is the top fleet degrader. 
  • Provide Supplier support - Support any RCCA investigations internal or with suppliers
  • Lead Reliability IPT for poor fielded reliability, multiple projects and efforts ongoing
  • Support Internal BAR/BUR meetings, Configuration Control Board, meets bi-weekly to review and release RNs and keep Engineering Metrics Repository up to date
  • Conduct Industry Team Base visits, go to bases and provide EODAS classes for maintainers, help troubleshoot aircraft with EODAS issues
  • Attend Production Review Boards bi-weekly,  focus on any technical efforts to keep yields high
  • Support Tactical Fighters EODAS Tech Road Map to include any necessary software updates

Basic Qualifications:  

  • Bachelor’s degree in a STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Math) discipline
  • Experience leading technical teams 
  • Experience in systems development, integration, and test  
  • Good communication skills 
  • 14 Years with Bachelors in Science; 12 Years with Masters; 9 Years with PhD
  • Active DoD Secret clearance

Preferred Qualifications:  

  • Master’s degree or PhD in STEM or technical management
  • Domain expertise in defense electronic systems
  • Understanding of DoD systems engineering requirements

Knowledge of earned value management
