The Aeronautics Systems (AS) sector has an opening for a Project Management/ Principal Project Management – Cost Analyst. This position will be located in Melbourne, Fl.

The Cost Analyst Project Manager will support programs in multiple phases of development, from early strategy to support capture to integration and post-production to support affordability assessments. Two areas of analysis that this role may be expected to perform are design to cost and life cycle cost estimation.

The design to cost (DTC) analysis will incorporate technical inputs to formulate DTC analysis approaches and methods to provide senior leadership the ability to make actionable decisions in a very agile manner. This work is responsible for decomposing top-level program cost targets by the WBS, assigning targets to the appropriate IPT (Integrated Product Team), working with the IPTs to identify and implement cost reduction opportunities to meet the cost targets, and tracking progress to the targets.

The lifecycle cost estimation (LCCE) analysis applies parametric estimating best practices and procedures to be able to take engineering data from the program and provide relevant and detailed parametric cost estimates to the program, strategy, and pricing and estimating teams. This work will be focused on problem solving through iteration to provide the key and relevant data to the strategy and leadership teams to ensure that program strategy leads to intelligent program execution and continues to solve problems throughout the program lifecycle. This analysis also develops a subsystem and detailed parametric cost estimate for a variety of potential solutions/scenarios to support different strategic program initiatives.

In addition, this position will also support implementation of other program affordability initiatives such as cost as an independent variable (CAIV), design for manufacturing & affordability, design for supportability, supply chain affordability and re-use & common product usage.  The position requires an understanding of life-cycle cost, schedule, risk, and affordability implications of alternatives under consideration.  The position requires ability to develop and oversee tradeoffs of requirements, design, performance, cost, and schedule to address affordability constraints. The position is responsible for encouraging and facilitating active participation of the broader stakeholder community and decision makers in exploring alternatives to achieve program affordability.
