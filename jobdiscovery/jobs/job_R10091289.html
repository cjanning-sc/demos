Senior Principal Mechanical Engineer - Subsystem Design Lead
R10091289
1st Shift (United States of America)

ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has an opening for Senior Principal Mechanical Engineer to lead our team of qualified diverse individuals in the Subsystem Design group. In Vehicle Engineering, we develop manned and unmanned aircraft platforms for all programs in the Aeronautics Systems Sector

This position will be located in Oklahoma City, OK and support the 9/80, 1st shift

We are looking for Engineers to develop “design for producibility” solutions to difficult problems that are thorough, imaginative, practical, and consistent with program objectives. In the Subsystems Design group, we develop solutions to difficult problems that are thorough, imaginative, practical, and consistent with program objectives. In your day-to-day activities you will participate in the development and review of completed models, drawings, and repairs for accuracy, clarity, and completeness, in order to provide evaluation of engineering products for conformity to standards, procedures, and specifications. You will also lead a team and supervise design solutions from initial concept to final implementation and give concise presentations and explanations of subsystem design issues/solutions to internal management and customer representatives.

Basic Qualifications:

  • BS degree in Mechanical Engineering, Aerospace Engineering, or Electrical Engineering and 9+ years of experience with a background in subsystem design (Masters degree may count towards an additional 2 years of experience).
  • Experience leading a group/team of engineers
  • High level of proficiency in a 3D CAD related software in the development of aircraft parts and assemblies.
  • Experience using CATIA V5
  • Working knowledge of one of the following subsystems: electrical, hydraulics, fuels, fluids, mechanisms or propulsion systems and/or experience with mechanical routing.
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret or Top Secret clearance.
  • Must have the ability to obtain and maintain Special Access Program (SAP) clearance.

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Advanced degree in Mechanical Engineering, Aerospace Engineering, or Electrical Engineering.
  • Experience with Model Based Engineering (MBE) or 3-D model engineering definition.
  • Experience with HarnesSys tool for schematic design.
  • Experience in the design of bonded composite primary and secondary aircraft structures.
  • Experience working on Special Access Programs
  • Experience working on military aircraft
  • Experience with subsystem integration
