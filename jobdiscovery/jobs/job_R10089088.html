Supply Chain Planning Specialist (Level 2 or 3)
R10089088
1st Shift (United States of America)
Aeronautics Systems sector is seeking a Supply Chain Planning Specialist (Level 2 or 3) to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position is located in Palmdale, CA.
  • 1st Shift, 3/10 schedule Friday - Sunday
  • Position is in-office, unable to accommodate a telecommute/hybrid schedule.

Essential Functions:

  • Defines and develops material strategy in tandem with manufacturing and production environments.
  • Responsible for the assessment of supply chain resources; aggregates and prioritizes demand requirements and the delivery for critical production parts.
  • Develops, coordinates, and executes material forecasting and part delivery schedules.
  • Participates in make/buy process, long-term capacity, and resource planning; product phase-in/phase-out, manufacturing ramp-up, end-of-life management and product-line management.
  • Participate in affordability, cost savings, and/or process improvement strategies.
  • Analyzes purchase material supply/demand position using Material Requirements Planning (MRP) tools.
  • Monitors inventory levels based on dependent and independent Bill-of-Materials (BOM) requirements.
  • Creates/reduces supply requirements based on inventory adjustments.
  • Participates in long-term capacity and resource planning to ensure proper scheduling of new product line phase-ins as well as existing product line phase-outs.
  • Interfaces with source process area regarding inventory levels, working capital and other areas as needed.
  • Receives/reviews requirements for non-standard parts.
  • Analyzes lead times and identifies critical path procurements.
  • Works closely with procurement team to coordinate/plan outside processing of parts/material with various suppliers/vendors, and coordinates/plans transportation of parts/material to/from vendor/supplier.
  • Supports manufacturing production providing planned and unplanned requirements in an expedited manner.
  • Collaborate cross functionally with Supplier Quality, Engineering, Quality, Manufacturing, and other support functions.
  • Prioritize and communicate impacts of current and potential material and part shortages. 
  • Operate company-owned vehicles to execute pick-up and delivery of parts between ACME Engineering sites/buildings, and offsite locations at times. 

Additional job duties include but are not limited to the following:

  • Work additional hours and/or days to support program needs when required. 
  • Assist team supply chain technical coordinators (material handling) when needed. Hybrid capacity (planning specialist/tech coordinator) when necessary.

Basic Qualifications (Level 2):

  • 7 years of experience with a HS diploma, 3 years of experience with a bachelor’s degree, or 1 year of experience with a master's degree. Experience must be in supply chain, procurement, logistics or another relevant field.
  • Familiar with Microsoft Suite with an emphasis on Excel and Power Point.
  • Proficient oral and written communication skills.
  • Must have developed organizational skills and the ability to handle multi-tasking in a high paced and dynamic environment.
  • Self-motivated and able to work as a teammate and independently.
  • Ability to obtain forklift certification.
  • Ability to perform 60% desk work and 40% production shop floor part integration responsibilities with the ability to lift, push, pull up to 30 pounds.
  • Ability to stand, walk and sit for prolonged periods of time during the assigned shift.
  • Valid driver’s license and ability to drive company-owned vehicles (potentially in the dark at times)
  • Ability to obtain and maintain DOD Secret Clearance and a Program Special Access within a reasonable period, as determined by the company to meet its business needs (after starting).
