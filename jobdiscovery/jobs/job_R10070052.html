ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems has an opening for an Obsolescence Engineer/ Obsolescence Principal Engineer to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals.  This position will be in Melbourne, FL.

 

Candidate will be responsible to perform full spectrum of Diminishing Manufacturing Sources and Material Shortages (DMSMS) management tasks on assigned system and sub-systems to ensure DMSMS risk areas are identified, and design influence is asserted to reduce and/ or remove obsolescence. Ability to develop/maintain an obsolescence bill of material, provide health screening, identify issues, perform obsolescence forecasting, lifetime buy/bridge buy quantity calculations, issue resolution development (through research and work with Design Engineering on the development of design solutions that align with the DMSMS roadmap) and issue management through solution implementation.  

 

Candidate will demonstrate a complete understanding and wide application of technical principle, theories, and concepts relative to DMSMS and life cycle management. Candidate will demonstrate general knowledge of aircraft and/or aircraft subsystem development, and familiarity with supplier management. Applicant is expected to work independently to provide innovative technical solutions to a wide range of difficult problems.

 

Candidate will create, analyze, and produce DMSMS technology roadmaps, impact, and issue reports and present data to stakeholders, customers, and upper management.  Applicant will be expected to maintain close coordination with several integrated product teams, provide detailed status to program leadership, participate in DMSMS management technical interchange meetings, as required.   Candidate will be self-motivated, work under general direction, and work well in teams.  Proficiency in Microsoft Office tools is essential.  Candidate must be able to obtain and sustain a Secret Security Clearance.

 

**This position may be filled by Obsolescence Engineer or Obsolescence Principal Engineer level based on the qualifications listed. **

 

Basic Qualifications Obsolescence Engineer:

· Bachelor’s Degree in a Science, Technology, Engineering or Mathematics discipline from an accredited university with 2 years’ experience; or a Master’s degree in a STEM discipline with 0 years’ experience

· Experience in at least one of the following disciplines: logistics, engineering, supplier management or program management

· An active in-scope DoD Secret or Top-Secret clearance

· The ability to obtain program access

discipline from an accredited university with 5 years’ experience; or a Master’s degree in a STEM discipline with 3 years’ experience

· Experience in at least one of the following disciplines: logistics, engineering, supplier management or program management

· An active in-scope DoD Secret or Top-Secret clearance

· The ability to obtain program access

 

Preferred Qualifications:

· Knowledge of military systems

· SAP program clearance and program experience

· Experience in more than one logistics discipline

· Experience in engineering, supplier management, or program management

· Excellent communication, interpersonal skills, and the ability to interface with all levels of employees and management
