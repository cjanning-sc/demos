ACME Engineering Aeronautics Systems (NGAS) is hiring! Join our team a Receiving Inspector – Level 2 based out of Clearfield, UT.

If selected, final clearance must be obtained prior to start.

Essential Functions:

  • Reads and interprets materials, such as work orders, inspection manuals, and blueprints, to determine inspection and test procedures
  • Inspects materials, products, and work in progress for conformance to specifications, and adjusts process or assembly equipment to meet standards
  • Analyzes and interprets sample data
  • Confers with vendors and others regarding inspection results, recommends corrective procedures, and compiles reports of results, recommendations, and needed repairs
  • Measures finished products, components, or assemblies for functioning, operation, accuracy, or assembly to verify adherence to functional specifications
  • Marks items for acceptance or rejection, records test results and inspection data, and compares findings with specifications to ensure conformance to standards
  • Inspect purchased materials for conformity to standards, specifications, using purchase quality codes, drawings, inspection plans, and checklists to verify Visual, dimensional Inspect, part marking, lot/serial numbers, lot quantity, handling and shipping damage
  • Perform Authorization to ship(ATS) document review of supplier’s documentation to ensure conformity to specifications and contractual requirements pre-shipment of material
  • Makes pass/fail decisions on inspected items, including documenting nonconformance in TipQA/(tag)  and material segregation
  • Perform verification of material temperature data and temperature requirements to ensure conformity to data records and time of receipt
  • Complete QCID, Solumina and CostPoint acceptance and print accept tags
  • Materials that require initial testing. Inspection will create lab Work Order and notify Stores/Inventory and Material Planner that the lab Work Order has been submitted to the lab. Material Planner shall authorize cutting personal to cut/pull material samples and physically move material samples to the Lab
  • Perform regular floor audits with operations, FOD walks

Basic Qualifications:

  • High School Diploma or GED with at least 2-years of manufacturing/production/inspection experience (or similar)​
  • Ability to obtain and maintain a DoD Secret Security Clearance and special program access within a reasonable period of time as determined by the company to meet its business needs

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active DoD Secret level security clearance
  • Composite experience and/or certification
  • Previous composite, manufacturing, or production experience
  • Demonstrated ability using standard measuring equipment and power hand tools
  • Ability to read and understand blueprints, technical specifications and operating procedures
