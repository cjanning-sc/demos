Supplier Quality Analyst (Level 2 or 3)
R10061206
1st Shift (United States of America)

Aeronautics Systems sector is seeking a Supplier Quality Analyst OR Principal Supplier Quality Analyst to join our team of qualified, diverse individuals. This position will be located in Clearfield, UT.

Essential Functions:

  • Plans, organizes, directs and reports on all supplier quality-related activities such as raw materials, packaging materials, good manufacturing practices (GMP) service providers, as well as contract manufacturers, and/or contract manufacturing and laboratories to assure fulfillment of contract/purchase order requirements.
  • Translates engineering, manufacturing, and quality requirements.
  • Assures that customer quality-imposed technical requirements are adhered to by supplier and that a quality system is maintained.
  • Conducts programs designed to improve supplier performance, productivity, and process validation.
  • Prepares, maintains and reviews procurement quality assurance procedures to assure compliance with customer and/or government requirements.
  • Reviews and analyzes corrective action reports and purchase orders in an effort to reduce and eliminate defects.
  • Monitors quality control activities and systems at supplier and subcontractor facilities, and maintains feedback loops.
  • May administer a certified supplier program or related programs in receiving inspection.
  • May also audit third party manufacturers, due diligence and vendor selection process.
  • May perform deviation investigations into quality issues.
  • Develop and implement program quality plans, programs, and procedures using statistical quality control statistics, lean manufacturing concepts, and six-sigma tools and analyses.
  • Reviews database design and integration of systems, provides backup recovery and makes recommendations regarding enhancements and/or improvements.
  • Acts as an advisor to senior management and customers.
  • Develops and implements long-term supplier quality strategies and networks that address capability issues, product nonconformance decisions, data analysis, metrics, etc. in support of business growth.
  • Interacts with senior internal and external personnel on significant matters often requiring coordination between organizations.
  • Represents organization as a prime contact on contracts or projects.
  • Exerts some influence on the overall objectives and long-range goals of the organization.

This role may be filled at a Level 2 or 3 based on the qualifications outlined below.

Basic Qualifications:

Supplier Quality Analyst (Level 2)

  • Bachelor's degree with 3 or more years of engineering, manufacturing, production control, and/or quality; OR a Master’s Degree with 1 or more years of engineering, manufacturing, or production control, and/or quality OR 7 or more years of relevant engineering, manufacturing, production control and/or quality experience in lieu of degree.
  • Knowledge of GD&T, Engineering Specifications, and manufacturing processing
  • Nonconformance Processing, MRB
  • Ability to obtain and maintain an active DoD Secret Clearance

Principal Supplier Quality Analyst (Level 3)

  • Bachelor's degree with 6 or more years of engineering, manufacturing, production control, and/or quality; OR a Master’s Degree in with 4 or more years of engineering, manufacturing, or production control, and/or quality OR 10 or more years of relevant engineering, manufacturing, production control and/or quality experience in lieu of degree.
  • Knowledge of GD&T, Engineering Specifications, and manufacturing processing
  • Nonconformance Processing, MRB
  • Ability to obtain and maintain an active DoD Secret Clearance

Preferred Qualifications:

  • Active DoD Secret Security Clearance
  • Ability to develop and deliver presentations
  • Experience with implementing and maintains of databases i.e. TipQA, Costpoint, Solumina
  • At least 1 year of demonstrated experience leading project teams
  • Strong expertise using the full suite of Microsoft Office Tools
  • Experience with AS9102 First Article Inspections.
