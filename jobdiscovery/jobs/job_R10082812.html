ACME Engineering Space Systems designs, builds and delivers space, and defense related systems to customers around the world. Our main products include launch vehicles and related propulsion systems; missile products, subsystems and defense electronics; precision weapons, armament systems and ammunition; satellites and associated space components and services; and advanced aerospace structures.

ACME Engineering Space System Sector is looking for a Staff Engineer Structural to join the Structural Analysis and Test team within Launch Vehicles at the Chandler, AZ facility.

Essential Functions:

  • Under minimal direction, perform a variety of structural engineering tasks of an analysis, design, or test nature
  • Perform stress analysis of metallic or composite primary and secondary structures
  • Optimize structures for mass using a variety of methods including topology optimization
  • Select and/or modify proven methods, mathematical formulas, previous design and test information, handbook data or other available information related to the assignment
  • Stress and bolted joint analysis of ground support equipment, test fixtures, compliance structures, and flight structures
  • Use classical stress and strain methods and software to predict structural integrity and failure and design flight components and ground equipment to survive worst-case loading conditions
  • Learn and develop proficiency with structural analysis software (Femap, NX Nastran, etc)
  • Interpret, select, and apply company/commercial/military specifications and customer requirements to accomplish specific analyses
  • Produce engineering documentation and technical reports/analyses to support design, integration, and testing efforts
  • Gather, compile, and interpret technical data such as flight data, test data, or material test reports
  • Other duties upon request

Basic Qualifications: 

  • Bachelor's degree in a STEM discipline (Science, Technology, Engineering or Math) with 14 years of related engineering experience; OR a Master’s degree in STEM with 12 years of related engineering experience, or a PhD in STEM with 9 years of related engineering experience
  • Ability to obtain a DoD Secret security clearance
  • Strong structural analysis background with demonstrated work experience and knowledge as a structural analyst
  • Ability to read engineering drawings and specifications
  • Experience with finite element analysis, classical stress and strain methodology, engineering statics, and structural mechanics
  • Computer skills with standard word processing/spreadsheet experience
  • Ability to listen effectively, take direction, and possess initiative to accomplish assigned tasks and complete work in a timely fashion


Preferred Qualifications: 

  • Master’s Degree in Mechanical/Aerospace principles
  • Active DoD Secret security clearance, or higher
  • Experience working on, and analyzing launch vehicle structures and ground support equipment, etc.
  • NX Nastran, Abaqus or equivalent
  • Femap, Patran or equivalent
  • Python, Matlab, or equivalent
